---

# 📌 **1. Player Flow (Step-by-Step)**

### **A. Player selects a quest**

* Each quest has:

  * *recommended rank*
  * *location*
  * *monster table*
  * *time requirements*
  * *danger rating*

### **B. Player chooses the adventure duration**

Options:

```
15 minutes
30 minutes
1 hour
2 hours
4 hours
```

### **C. Idle combat begins**

* No turn-by-turn commands from the player
* The bot simulates continuous encounters
* Logs are posted **every 5 minutes** in a channel or DM

### **D. If the player dies → adventure ends early**

A death summary appears immediately.

### **E. If they survive → final summary + rewards**

Exp, gold, materials, items, quest progression.

# 📌 **5. Idle Combat Logic**

Each 5-minute cycle:

### Step 1 — determine encounter:

* Use location monster table (weighted %)
* Maybe spawn elites (5%) or boss (0.5%)

### Step 2 — simulate the entire fight instantly:

* Combat engine processes:

  * Damage per round
  * Crit chance
  * Monster actions
  * Phrases (narration)
* Stop if the player’s HP = 0

### Step 3 — store the logs (DB or memory)

### Step 4 — send the log to Discord

### Step 5 — reduce remaining adventure time

### Step 6 — end session if:

* Time = 0
* Player died

# 📌 **8. Failure Conditions**

If the player dies:

* All progress stops
* They lose a percentage of gold (configurable)
* They gain **reduced** EXP
* The Guildmaster comments (harshly or sympathetically)

Example:

> “A poor showing, adventurer. Even fledgling goblins should not be your undoing.”
> — *Daemon Caelungarde*

---

# 📌 **9. Success Rewards**

At the end:

* EXP from all monsters
* Gold rewards
* Chance for item drops
* Quest progression

High LCK increases:

* Rare drops
* Set item drops
* Critical encounter spawns
