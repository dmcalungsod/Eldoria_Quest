# 🌑 **Eldoria Quest — Core Theme & Creative Direction**

### **High Fantasy • Dark, Atmospheric • Anime-adventurer Inspired • Book-like Prose**

Eldoria Quest takes place in a world shaped by **ancient calamity and fractured magic**. After the Sundering split the Veil between realms, creatures of nightmare, displaced spirits, and forgotten gods now walk the land freely. Civilization clings to fragile islands of safety—city-states lit by runic wards, adventurer guilds built out of desperation, and roads patrolled only by the brave or the foolish.

This world’s tone blends:

* **Dark High Fantasy** — curses, broken kingdoms, old magic, corrupted landscapes
* **Anime Adventurer Aesthetic** — guilds, ranks, quests, personal-growth arcs
* **Book-like, literary narration** — descriptive, atmospheric, Game-of-Thrones-like worldbuilding

Your bot and game systems should feel like a **story being told**, not just numbers being processed.

---

# 🖋 **Narrative Style Guidelines**

All text generated for the project—class descriptions, combat messages, item flavor, guild ranks—should follow these rules:

### **1. Descriptive, atmospheric, immersive**

Use sensory detail (sight, sound, magic, mood):

> “The wind carries the stink of corrupted soil as the beast lunges.”

### **2. High fantasy tone**

Use archaic or elegant wording when appropriate, but keep it readable:

> “Forged in the age before light, the blade hums with quiet hunger.”

### **3. Slight anime-adventure flair**

Energetic, heroic, guild-like systems:

> “Your guild tag glows faintly as you accept your first contract.”

### **4. Avoid silliness unless explicitly intended**

The world is not comedic—danger and mystery dominate.

### **5. Always remain lore-consistent**

Everything should feel like it belongs in the same world.

---

# ⚒ **System & Code Expectations**

❗ These rules are for your AI Assistant (e.g., Gemini CLI) to follow when generating or editing your game’s Python code.

### **1. Code must be clean, fully indented, and error-free**

* Proper imports
* Respect package structure
* No missing commas/colons
* No broken indentation after editing

### **2. Comments explain purpose but don’t bloat**

Docstrings for modules, classes, and methods.

### **3. Text content (flavor, messages, dialogs) follows Eldoria’s dark-fantasy tone**

All output text must fit the narrative style described above.

### **4. SQLite-safe strings and formatting**

Avoid breaking SQL with quotes.

### **5. Modular structure**

Every engine or system kept in its own module:

* `damage_formula.py`
* `exp_calculator.py`
* `gold_rewards.py`
* `monster_actions.py`
* `combat_phrases.py`
* `combat_engine.py` integrating them

### **6. The bot behaves like an Adventurer Guild System**

For players, your bot **is** the Guild of Eldoria.

# 🛡 **Guild System Tone (Very Important)**

The guild is:

* Ancient
* Structured
* Bureaucratic, but mystical
* The last bastion against the chaos beyond

Whenever users interact with the bot (character creation, rank-up, quest acceptance), it should feel like dealing with a **guild official**:

> “Your name is inked into the Guild Registry.
> May your steps tread boldly where others falter.”
