# Region Design: Ironhaven

## Core Identity
- **Name:** Ironhaven
- **Theme:** Militant, fortified mountain city that values strength, discipline, and heavy industry. The atmosphere is stoic, cold, and unyielding.
- **History:** Established shortly after The Sundering by a splinter group of the Iron Vanguard, Ironhaven was carved directly into the flanks of Mount Kaelen to withstand the onslaught of airborne Void horrors. It serves as Eldoria's northern shield.
- **Culture:** A spartan society where martial prowess and metallurgy are revered. Every citizen undergoes combat training, and the hum of the forges never stops.

## Geography & Locations
- **The Aegis Gates:** Colossal iron double-doors guarding the only terrestrial entrance, constantly besieged by mountain beasts.
- **The Deep Forges:** The industrial heart of Ironhaven, tapping into geothermal vents and powered by massive, stable magic stones to smelt the toughest ores.
- **The Aviary Towers:** High-altitude, wind-battered platforms used by drake-riders to patrol the skies and hunt aerial monsters before they can descend on Astraeon.
- **The Howling Peaks:** The treacherous, snowy mountains surrounding the city, home to deadly creatures and hidden, frozen ruins.

## Inhabitants
- **Factions:** Iron Vanguard (Northern Chapter), The Forge-Masters (artisan guild), The Drake-Riders (sky cavalry).
- **Notable NPCs:**
  - **General Rask:** The battle-scarred commander of the Vanguard chapter, stern and pragmatic.
  - **Magni the Anvil:** Head of the Forge-Masters, a master smith who works exclusively with Star Metal.
- **Monster Ecology:** Storm Drakes (nesting in the high peaks), Iron-clad Trolls (wandering the lower slopes), Frost Gargoyles (ambushing travelers on the mountain paths).

## Gameplay Elements
- **Access Requirements:** Rank A or completion of the "The Northern Shield" questline.
- **Exploration Mechanics:** Cold and Altitude management. Players need warmth (e.g., thermal gear, specific potions) and must manage increased stamina drain due to thin air. Vertical navigation requiring grappling hooks.
- **Quests & Storylines:**
  - "The Sky is Falling": Defend the Aviary Towers from a massive aerial Void assault.
  - "The Broken Anvil": Secure rare Frost-forged Steel to repair the main Deep Forge.
- **Resources & Economy:** Star Metal, Frost-forged Steel, Drake Scales, and Glacial Hearts.
- **Dangers & Challenges:** Freezing winds that deal damage over time without proper gear, avalanches, and ambushes by intelligent, coordinated Frost Gargoyles.

## Integration with Existing Systems
- **DepthsWarden:** Potential connection from the Deep Forges into a new dungeon floor or abandoned mines.
- **GameForge:** Needs to create the new monsters (Storm Drakes, Iron-clad Trolls, Frost Gargoyles), resources (Frost-forged Steel, Drake Scales), and quests.
- **StoryWeaver:** Write stoic, militant dialogue for General Rask and atmospheric descriptions for the biting cold of the Howling Peaks.
- **Namewright:** Provide names for specific peaks, localized factions, and key Drake-Rider NPCs.
- **Grimwarden:** Balance the Cold/Altitude survival mechanics to ensure they are punishing but fair.
- **GameBalancer:** Balance the economic impact of Star Metal and Frost-forged Steel.
- **ProgressionBalancer:** Ensure Rank A is the correct requirement and scales well with players reaching Level 30+.
- **Tactician:** Design combat behaviors for aerial enemies and coordinated Gargoyle attacks.
- **ChronicleKeeper:** Add achievements like "Mountain Conqueror" and "Ironclad Defender".
- **Equipper:** Design thermal gear and grappling hook items required for safe navigation.
