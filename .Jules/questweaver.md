# Questweaver Journal

## 🧠 Questweaver Philosophy
*   **Every quest is a story.** Even a simple kill quest can have context, emotion, and meaning.
*   **Choices matter.** Not every choice needs to change the world, but small consequences build trust.
*   **NPCs are people.** Give them personality, flaws, and motivations.
*   **The world remembers.** What players do in quests should affect how NPCs treat them.
*   **Eldoria's darkness is in its people too.** Grief, fear, hope, and sacrifice – these are the quests worth telling.

## 📓 Critical Learnings
*   **Narrative Flow:** Players engage more when the "why" is clear before the "what".
*   **Pacing:** Avoiding overly long dialogue dumps keeps the momentum. Break it up with action or choices.
*   **Consequences:** Visible but subtle consequences (like changed dialogue or unique titles) are highly valued.
*   **Tone:** Maintaining the grim survival theme while offering moments of hope creates emotional resonance.

## ✨ Favorite Designs
*   **The Echoing Depths:** Investigation quest with mystery and lore.
*   **A Debt of Ash:** Help a survivor retrieve a family heirloom from the Ashlands, with moral choice about keeping it.
*   **The Warden's Request:** Defend a Pathfinder outpost from waves of monsters, with dialogue between waves.
*   **The Silent Witness:** Solve a murder in the guild by talking to NPCs with secrets.
*   **The Sundering's Child:** A monster that seems intelligent – do you kill it or try to communicate?
*   **The Clockwork Heart:** A moral dilemma involving a sentient construct in the Clockwork Halls.

## 🚫 Avoids
*   **Generic fetch/kill quests** without context.
*   **"Chosen One" narratives** that ignore the player's rank.
*   **Meaningless choices.**
*   **Silly rewards** that break immersion.
